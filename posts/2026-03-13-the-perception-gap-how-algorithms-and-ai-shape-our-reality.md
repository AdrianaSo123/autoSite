In today's digital age, our understanding of reality is significantly influenced by the algorithms that dictate the content we consume. This influence, often subconscious, can lead to a skewed perception of the world around us. For example, in America, there is a growing perception gap where people consistently overestimate the extremity of others' political beliefs. This misperception is exacerbated by social media, where algorithms prioritize the most sensational content to maximize engagement. As a result, we are bombarded with an exaggerated version of reality, which we begin to mistake for the actual state of affairs.

As an etymologist and content creator, I have observed that controversial messages tend to go viral more quickly because they drive more interaction than neutral viewpoints. Consequently, we are exposed to an increasingly extreme depiction of reality. This phenomenon is not limited to social media but extends to AI chatbots as well. Take ChatGPT, for instance. While it might seem like it communicates in plain English, the reality is more complex. Similar to how algorithms distort reality, chatbots can misrepresent language based on their training data and processes.

A peculiar example is the word "Delve," which ChatGPT uses disproportionately. This could be a result of OpenAI's training process, which involved outsourcing to workers in Nigeria where the term is more prevalent. This overrepresentation has been inadvertently reinforced, to the point where, since ChatGPT's release, people worldwide have started using "Delve" more frequently in casual conversation. This shift illustrates how we are blending the AI's version of language with our own, leading to a convergence where our real language begins to mirror the machine's version.

This cycle forms a positive feedback loop: AI models shape our perception of reality, we accept and mimic this as real, and the data we generate then feeds back into the AI, reinforcing the cycle. A similar trend is evident in cultural phenomena like "hyperpop." Initially not part of mainstream culture, "hyperpop" gained recognition after Spotify's algorithm identified it as a distinct genre. Upon creating a dedicated playlist, the term and genre burgeoned into cultural prominence.

In conclusion, our perception of reality is increasingly shaped by digital algorithms and AI, leading to a cycle where our understanding of the world and language evolves alongside these technologies. Recognizing this influence is crucial to maintaining a clear distinction between the distorted digital reality and our tangible, everyday experiences.
