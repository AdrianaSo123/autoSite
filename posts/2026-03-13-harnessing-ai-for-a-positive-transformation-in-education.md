In recent months, we have been seeing headlines discussing the impact of artificial intelligence, particularly in education. The prevailing thesis is that students will use tools like ChatGPT and other AI technologies to cheat on their assignments, potentially undermining the learning process as we know it. 

However, I want to argue that not only are there ways to mitigate these concerns with the right guardrails and strategies, but we are also on the brink of using AI for perhaps the most significant positive transformation that education has ever seen. 

By implementing AI correctly, we can provide every student on the planet with an artificially intelligent, yet exceptional, personal tutor. Additionally, we can equip every teacher with a remarkable AI teaching assistant. This advancement would be monumental, fundamentally changing the educational landscape and enhancing the learning experience for both students and educators worldwide.
